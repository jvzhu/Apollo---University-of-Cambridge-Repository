# Cambridge Research Notes — Vivien Jiaqian Zhu 朱嘉倩

## Overview

This repository documents research materials, annotation exercises, and machine learning coursework connected to my Cambridge fellowship, my work as a research annotator on the **FigurativeAccess** project (Cambridge Language Sciences / AI@Cam Incubator Fund), and independent study supporting my broader research on LLM benchmarking for classical Chinese literature.

It is a personal working repository, not an official University of Cambridge or Apollo (apollo.repository.cam.ac.uk) resource. Cambridge's institutional repository for theses and publications is Apollo, hosted at https://www.repository.cam.ac.uk/ — this repo is named informally and independently, and is not affiliated with or endorsed by that service.

## About Me

Ph.D. candidate and Regent's Fellow in Japanese Literature, UC Berkeley Department of East Asian Languages and Cultures. Visiting scholar affiliation at Stanford University; fellow at the University of Cambridge. ## Contents

### Cambridge-affiliated research

**FigurativeAccess: Building Resources and Tools to Make Figurative Language More Accessible**
Research annotator (pilot phase), Cambridge Language Sciences Incubator Fund / AI@Cam "AI-deas" collaboration.
Project team: Songqiao Xie, Dr Xinxin Yan (UCL), Dr Mario Giulianelli (UCL), Prof. Napoleon Katsos, Dr Miloš Stanojević (Google DeepMind).
My role: native-speaker annotation of figurative language (metaphor, metonymy, hyperbole, simile) in British English corpus excerpts.

### Independent annotation exercises

- `cliffe_kimono_annotation.md`, `cliffe_kimono_annotation-2.md` — annotation notes connected to Japanese fashion/kimono research
- `pelea_lolita_annotation.md` — annotation notes on Lolita fashion subculture

### Machine learning coursework

A set of exercise notebooks (Kaggle Learn: Intro to Machine Learning / Intro to Deep Learning) — arithmetic and variables, functions, model validation, data leakage, XGBoost, custom convnets, data augmentation, sliding window, and a general working notebook. These support the technical groundwork for my recension-aware LLM benchmark project on *Jin Ping Mei* (distinguishing *cihua* and *xiuxiang* textual recensions and detecting anachronistic commentary importation).
