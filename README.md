# Cambridge Research Notes — Vivien Jiaqian Zhu 朱嘉倩

## Overview

This repository documents research materials, correspondence records, and project notes connected to my affiliations with the University of Cambridge, including my fellowship and my work as a research annotator on the **FigurativeAccess** project (Cambridge Language Sciences / AI@Cam Incubator Fund, Dept. of Theoretical and Applied Linguistics).

It is a personal working repository, not an official University of Cambridge or Apollo (apollo.repository.cam.ac.uk) resource. Cambridge's institutional repository for theses and publications is Apollo, hosted at https://www.repository.cam.ac.uk/ — this repo is named informally and independently, and is not affiliated with or endorsed by that service.

## About Me

Ph.D. candidate and Regent's Fellow in Japanese Literature, UC Berkeley Department of East Asian Languages and Cultures. Visiting scholar affiliation at Stanford University; fellow at the University of Cambridge. Research spans Ming-Qing fiction commentary (*Jin Ping Mei* and the Zhang Zhupo 1695 commentarial tradition), comparative poetics, and Japanese fashion studies.

## Current Cambridge-Affiliated Work

### FigurativeAccess: Building Resources and Tools to Make Figurative Language More Accessible
Research annotator (pilot phase), Cambridge Language Sciences Incubator Fund / AI@Cam "AI-deas" collaboration.
Project team: Songqiao Xie, Dr Xinxin Yan (UCL), Dr Mario Giulianelli (UCL), Prof. Napoleon Katsos, Dr Miloš Stanojević (Google DeepMind).
My role: native-speaker annotation of figurative language (metaphor, metonymy, hyperbole, simile) in British English corpus excerpts, contributing to a benchmark comparing human and AI-model identification of figurative language.
